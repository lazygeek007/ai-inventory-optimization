Core Business Findings
📦
Systemic Under-stocking Across All Five Categories
The notebook's inventory classification (df_final) finds all five categories at Low Stock status. Average inventory (~270 units) is well below reorder points derived at 95% service level (z=1.65, lead_time=7). The notebook explicitly prints: "5 Low Stock | 0 Safe Stock out of 5 categories." This indicates the retailer operates without demand-variability buffers, increasing stockout exposure during demand spikes and promotional periods.
📈
ARIMA Outperforms Both Prophet and LSTM on This Dataset
ARIMA (5,1,0) achieves the lowest MAE and RMSE on the 30-day test window. The notebook selects the best model programmatically and confirms ARIMA. LSTM underperforms despite higher computational cost — the notebook attributes this to limited training data (~700 days) and the relatively linear nature of demand patterns. The notebook conclusion is explicit: "ARIMA has the lowest MAE and RMSE, indicating higher prediction accuracy." Prophet performs second-best and is recommended for seasonal decomposition and interpretability.
🎯
Promotions Have a Statistically Significant Impact on Sales
The independent t-test (t=4.82, p < 0.001) and Cohen's d=0.41 confirm a medium-magnitude promotional uplift. The notebook also computes per-category uplift percentages and visualises them as a horizontal bar chart. The finding supports pre-stocking before promotional events, particularly for Electronics and Clothing which show the highest category-level promotional sensitivity.
💰
Estimated ₹14+ Lakh Annual Value Opportunity
The notebook's financial quantification cell uses: avg_daily_sales × avg_price (₹500 fallback), 30 stockout days, 25% reduction → stockout revenue recovery. Plus EOQ ordering efficiency (30% over-ordering baseline vs optimal). The notebook prints these figures directly. Total combined saving is estimated conservatively. These are model-based estimates using dataset averages, not audited financials.
⚖️
Service Level vs Holding Cost — Quantified Trade-off
The sensitivity analysis loop (service_levels dict: 80%, 90%, 95%, 99%) computes safety stock, ROP, inventory gap, and units to order for each level and category. Results are pivoted and printed. Moving from 80% to 99% service level increases safety stock by approximately 177%. The 80%→95% step captures most stockout protection at substantially lower incremental cost — the efficient frontier for procurement planning.
🔧
EOQ Calculated at ₹50 Ordering Cost, ₹2 Holding Cost
The notebook uses ordering_cost=50 and holding_cost=2 as MBA standard assumptions. EOQ = √(2 × annual_demand × 50 / 2) ≈ 1,578+ units per order cycle (depending on average daily sales). The notebook confirms: "Ordering approximately 1,578 units per cycle helps maintain cost efficiency." This corrects the ₹500/₹20 values that appear in prior versions of the dashboard.

Dataset Justification
Why Simulated Data?
Real-world retail inventory datasets are frequently proprietary and subject to NDA constraints. The notebook explicitly states: "Data represents multi-category retail environment. Demand follows seasonal patterns. Inventory decisions are made at category level." The simulation assumptions are consistent with supply chain analytics literature (Silver, Pyke & Thomas, 2017; Chopra & Meindl, 2021). All methodology generalises to real datasets.
Limitations & Future Scope
▸Optimization at category level, not SKU — future work should disaggregate
▸LSTM requires larger data volumes (3+ years) to outperform ARIMA reliably
▸Promotions/pricing not yet used as exogenous regressors in the forecast models
▸Reinforcement learning for adaptive reorder decisions not implemented
▸Real-time ERP integration required for production deployment
